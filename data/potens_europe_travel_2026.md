# Potens European Operations Travel and Expense Policy (2026)

**Document ID**: `potens-europe-travel`  
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2026  
**Scope**: All employees of Potens IT Services and Consultancy working under the European Operations entity.

---

## 1. Flight Travel Guidelines

### 1.1 Intra-Europe Flights
Employees must book Economy Class for all flights within Europe (durations under 6 hours). Premium Economy or Business Class upgrades are not reimbursable for intra-Europe travel.

### 1.2 Transcontinental Flights
Business Class travel is permitted only for transcontinental flights exceeding 6 hours (e.g., London to Mumbai, Frankfurt to New York). Any exceptions must be approved in writing by the European Operations Managing Director.

### 1.3 Train Preference
For travel between cities connected by high-speed rail with a total journey time of under 4 hours (e.g., Paris to Brussels, Brussels to London), employees must book train travel (Standard Class) instead of flying.

---

## 2. Lodging and Accommodation

### 2.1 Hotel Capping
Reimbursement for hotel accommodation is capped in Euros (€):
- **Western Europe (Tier 1)** (London, Paris, Geneva, Munich, Amsterdam): Maximum reimbursement is **€180 per night** (excluding local taxes).
- **Eastern & Central Europe (Tier 2)** (Warsaw, Budapest, Prague, etc.): Maximum reimbursement is **€120 per night** (excluding local taxes).

---

## 3. Meal Allowances and Daily Per Diem

### 3.1 Per Diem Limits
Meal expenses are reimbursed based on a flat daily allowance in Euros (€):
- **Western Europe (Tier 1)**: Daily meal allowance is capped at **€60 per day**.
- **Eastern & Central Europe (Tier 2)**: Daily meal allowance is capped at **€40 per day**.

### 3.2 Receipt Requirements
Receipts are required for all meal expenses. If a dinner is hosted for client relationship purposes, actual expenses may exceed the cap but must be approved by the Country Manager.

---

## 4. Ground Transportation

### 4.1 Local Commute
Public transport (train, tram, metro) is the preferred method of ground transit. Taxi or ridesharing (e.g. FreeNow, Uber) is permissible when traveling with luggage or during late hours (after 9:00 PM).

### 4.2 Personal Vehicle Mileage
Employees using their personal vehicles for business travel are eligible for mileage reimbursement at a rate of **€0.40 per kilometer** (please note this is measured in kilometers, not miles).

---

## 5. Approvals and Submission Timelines

### 5.1 Submission Window
All travel expense claims must be submitted via the EU SAP portal within **30 days** of the travel end date.

### 5.2 Approval Matrix
- Claims under €1,000: Require direct Manager approval.
- Claims exceeding €1,000: Require Manager and Country Manager approval.
- Claims exceeding €5,000: Require European Operations Managing Director approval.
