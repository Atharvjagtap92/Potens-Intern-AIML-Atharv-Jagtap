# Potens Consulting Travel and Expense Policy (2026)

**Document ID**: `potens-consulting-travel`  
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2026  
**Scope**: All consultants, project managers, and partners of Potens Consulting Division

---

## 1. Flight Travel Guidelines

### 1.1 Client-Billable Travel
For projects where travel costs are fully billed back to the client:
- **Business Class** is permitted for all domestic and international flights, regardless of duration, *only if* the client agreement explicitly authorizes Business Class travel and the client has provided written approval of the budget prior to booking.
- If the client contract does not support business class, consultants must follow the Core Policy.

### 1.2 Non-Billable (Internal) Travel
For internal company travel (not billable to any client), consultants must adhere to the standard Core Travel Policy: Economy Class for domestic flights and Business Class only for international flights over 8 hours.

---

## 2. Lodging and Accommodation

### 2.1 Hotel Capping
- **Client-Billable Travel**: Accommodation is capped at **$250 per night** (excluding taxes) in all locations, provided the client has agreed to lodging expenses. Any hotel selection above $250 requires written client permission.
- **Non-Billable Travel**: Capped at **$150 per night** for Tier 1 cities and **$100 per night** for other locations.

---

## 3. Meal Allowances and Daily Per Diem

### 3.1 Client-Billable Travel Meals
Meal expenses incurred during client-billable engagements are reimbursed at **actual cost** (no flat per diem cap) and will be billed directly to the client. Consultants are expected to exercise professional judgment. All claims must be backed by itemized receipts.

### 3.2 Non-Billable Travel Meals
For internal engagements, a flat daily allowance of **$60 per day** applies, regardless of the city tier.

---

## 4. Ground Transportation

### 4.1 Client-Billable Commutes
Premium ride options (e.g., Uber Black, local executive cars) are permissible for client-billable travel if they facilitate working en route or client meetings, subject to client billing terms.

### 4.2 Personal Vehicle Mileage
Consultants using their personal vehicles for business travel are eligible for mileage reimbursement at a rate of **$0.50 per mile**.

---

## 5. Approvals and Submission Timelines

### 5.1 Submission Window
Due to client billing cycles, expense claims must be submitted within **14 days** of the travel end date. Late submissions will not be billed to the client and may be deducted from the consultant's department budget or denied.

### 5.2 Approval Matrix
All expense claims require approval from the **Engagement Manager** (EM) and the **Client Account Director** (CAD).
