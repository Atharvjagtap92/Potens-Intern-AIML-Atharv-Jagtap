# Potens Foundation Travel and Expense Policy (2026)

**Document ID**: `potens-foundation-travel`  
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2026  
**Scope**: All staff, field workers, and volunteers of the Potens Foundation (Non-profit Division)

---

## 1. Flight Travel Guidelines

### 1.1 Air Travel Restrictions
All employees and volunteers traveling on behalf of the Potens Foundation must fly Economy Class. **Business Class and Premium Economy are strictly prohibited** for all flights, both domestic and international, regardless of duration or distance. 

### 1.2 Alternative Transportation
For journeys under 6 hours (door-to-door), staff are strongly encouraged to use rail travel (trains) instead of flights to reduce carbon emissions and minimize costs.

---

## 2. Lodging and Accommodation

### 2.1 Hotel Capping
Reimbursement for hotel accommodation is capped as follows:
- **Tier 1 Cities** (Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi NCR): Maximum reimbursement is **$100 per night** (excluding taxes).
- **Tier 2/3 Cities and Field Sites**: Maximum reimbursement is **$75 per night** (excluding taxes).
- Homestays or guesthouses should be preferred over commercial hotels where available.

---

## 3. Meal Allowances and Daily Per Diem

### 3.1 Per Diem Limits
Meal expenses are capped at:
- **Tier 1 Cities**: Daily meal allowance is capped at **$30 per day**.
- **Other Cities and Rural Sites**: Daily meal allowance is capped at **$20 per day**.

### 3.2 Reimbursement Exclusions
Under no circumstances will alcohol be reimbursed. Meal claims do not require individual receipts if within the flat per-diem limits, but they must be logged with date and purpose.

---

## 4. Ground Transportation

### 4.1 Public Transit Preference
Staff are expected to use public transportation (metro, public buses, local trains) wherever feasible and safe. Private taxis or auto-rickshaws are permitted only when carrying heavy field equipment, in cases of medical necessity, or when public transit is unavailable/unsafe.

### 4.2 Personal Vehicle Mileage
Mileage reimbursement for personal vehicle use is set at **$0.35 per mile**.

---

## 5. Approvals and Submission Timelines

### 5.1 Submission Window
Expense reports must be submitted within **15 days** of returning from travel.

### 5.2 Approval Matrix
All expense claims must be approved by the **Executive Director** (ED) of the Foundation. Any exception to lodging or transport caps must be approved in writing by the Board of Trustees prior to the trip.
